    Binchotan pressed coal

    Pressed coal, also known as coal briquettes, is a type of fuel that is made by compressing pulverized coal into a solid mass. It is a common alternative to traditional coal, as it burns more cleanly and efficiently. Pressed coal is created by mixing coal dust with a binding agent and then pressing the mixture into a mold to form a briquette. These briquettes are then dried and can be used as a fuel source for heating or cooking. Pressed coal has many benefits, including lower emissions, higher heat output, and reduced ash production. It is an excellent option for those looking for a cleaner and more sustainable fuel source.

    Black wood charcoal

    Lump charcoal, also known as black charcoal, is a type of charcoal made by burning wood in a low-oxygen environment. It is a popular choice for outdoor grilling and smoking due to its high heat output and low ash production. Lump charcoal is made from a variety of hardwoods, such as oak, hickory, and mesquite, and is typically sold in large, irregularly-shaped pieces. Its natural, smoky flavor makes it a favorite among BBQ enthusiasts, and its lack of additives or fillers means that it burns clean and hot, giving you the best possible cooking experience. Whether you're a seasoned pitmaster or a backyard BBQ enthusiast, lump charcoal is a must-try for anyone who loves great food cooked over an open flame.

    Bamboo Charcoal Takesumi

    Takesumi is a traditional Japanese bamboo charcoal that has become increasingly popular in recent years for its numerous health and beauty benefits. Made by burning bamboo at high temperatures, takesumi is known for its purifying properties and ability to detoxify the body. It is often used as an ingredient in skincare and beauty products due to its ability to absorb impurities, excess oil, and bacteria from the skin. Takesumi can also be consumed as a health supplement, as it is believed to aid digestion, boost the immune system, and promote overall wellness. In addition to its health benefits, takesumi is also used in cooking as a natural flavor enhancer and as a natural alternative to traditional charcoal briquettes for grilling. With its many benefits and versatility, takesumi is quickly becoming a popular choice for health-conscious consumers and chefs alike.
